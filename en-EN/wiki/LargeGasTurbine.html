Large Gas Turbine

The turbine is capable of generating energy, using gas. Accelerates slowly, used for continuous energy production.

Important!

The mechanics of work have been changed compared to the official version of GregTech, please read the information provided carefully.

Large Gas Turbine finished structure

A large gas turbine runs on a variety of gases.
The turbine requires a rotor to operate. There are many options available, differing in size, material, work efficiency and optimal flow modifier.

Important!

The rotor is disposable, that is, you insert it into the interface of the turbine controller, the turbine remembers the characteristics and remove the rotor, the characteristics are retained until you break the controller.

Efficiency:

Efficiency - percentage influencing turbine energy output.

Optimal flow:

Optimal Flow is the size of the flow required to achieve the optimal energy efficiency of the turbine. Each rotor has an individual optimal flow value.

Since the turbine can receive different gas, the optimal flow is indicated not in L/t, but in EU/t.

For gas, Actual Optimal Flow is used, performed by the formula:
Actual Optimal Flow (L/t) = Optimal Flow / (Fuel Value / 1000)

Fuel Value:

You can determine Fuel Value using NEI, find the fuel we need and look at the use in the turbine, it shows the energy content of one capsule.

Power generation:

Energy generation is performed according to the formula:
EU/t = Actual Optimal Flow * (Fuel Value / 1000) * (Efficiency / 100)

An example of generating energy from gas

Large Rotor made of Carbon with an efficiency of 120, with an optimal flow of 150 EU/t and Refinery Gas with an energy density of 128,000 EU per capsule.

Actual Optimal Flow:
150 / (128000 / 1000) = 1 L/t.

Power generation:
1 * (128000 / 1000) * (120 / 100) = 154 EU/t.
